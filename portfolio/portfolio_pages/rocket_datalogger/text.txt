Solving the hardware integration problem was only half the battle. In order to provide stable control to the reaction wheels, the orientation
                    data needs to be clean and free from noise. Additionally, the IMUs are only able to output acceleration data, not velocity. At this stage,
                    the project quickly became a control theory heavy crisis. I call it a crisis, because I am not an electrical engineer and don't do signal processing,
                    yet here I am. <br> <br>
                    <b> KALMAN FILTER </b> <br> <br>
                    To reduce noise from the rocket's inertial data, I opted to use a Kalman Filter. 
                    <br> <br>
                    <b> PID CONTROLLER </b> <br> <br>
                    A PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) controller is a feedback mechanism used to maintain/change a variable to a desired set point. In the context
                    of the project, this will be used for regulating the rocket's yaw axis stability by accelerating or decelerating the flywheel. This will also aid in
                    video stability and make it easier to decode the lights on the ground.
