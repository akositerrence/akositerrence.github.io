ABOUT

A junior at North Dakota State University, I'm pursuing a specialization in Aeronautical Engineering. Guided by interdisciplinarian principles and a deep passion for anything plane-related, I'm majoring in Mechanical Engineering with minors in Computer Science and Robotics. I thrive in collaborative, hands-on environments and enjoy tackling challenges at the intersection of hardware, software, and systems thinking. I will be graduating in the spring of 2027.

Born and raised in the Philippines, I lived in San Miguel, Bulacan and spent my summers in Singapore with my parents. When I was 13, we relocated to the United States, where I completed middle and high school in Bismarck, North Dakota, before moving to Fargo to continue my studies.

Currently, I'm an intern at NASA's Langley Research Center, supporting the Loads and Dynamics Technical Discipline Team in analyzing and assessing structural responses to dynamic forces. Prior to joining NASA, I gained experience in the composite materials industry through R&D support of the pultrusion process at Marvin Composites and in the agricultural technology sector piloting autonomous robots with Aigen.

Beyond my academic and professional responsibilities, I'm actively involved and serve as a leader in several college organizations such as ASME, AIAA, Bison Robotics, Climbing Club, and Chi Alpha. I spend most of my free time Rock Climbing, along with some lifting and running. I'm fluent in both English and Filipino, and I also play the clarinet, saxophone, and guitar.
